Ingredients:

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

For the Chicken Masala:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup fried onions
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • 2-3 black cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cinnamon sticks
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon saffron strands soaked in 1/4 cup milk
  • Chopped coriander leaves and mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

For the Rice:

  1. Wash the rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a large pot and add the cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, bay leaf, and salt.
  3. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water.
  4. Cook the rice until it is 70% done. Drain the water and keep the rice aside.

For the Chicken Masala:

  1. Marinate the chicken pieces in yogurt, fried onions, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, turmeric powder,
    red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala powder, and salt for 2-3 hours.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot and add the black cardamom pods, cinnamon sticks, cloves, and bay leaf.
  3. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. Add the marinated chicken and fry for 8-10 minutes on high heat.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot and cook for 15-20 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through.

To Assemble the Biryani:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180Â°C.
  2. Take a large oven-proof dish and grease it with some oil or ghee.
  3. Layer the rice and chicken masala in the dish, starting with a layer of rice at the bottom.
  4. Sprinkle some saffron milk on top of the rice and chicken.
  5. Repeat the layers until all the rice and chicken masala are used up.
  6. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30-35 minutes.
  7. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30-35 minutes.
  8. Garnish with chopped coriander and mint leaves.
  9. Serve hot with raita, salan, or any other side dish of your choice.
